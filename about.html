Who We Are

SafeStart Medical is the result of a career-long passion for patient safety. Despite health care information technology and the WHO Universal Protocol checklists, Never Events continue to occur at an alarming rate. We need to address medical system errors that cause great patient harm.

SafeStart is not just a checklist - it's a safety solution.

We drive the safety process from the front office through the operating room, engaging patients and staff along the way. Our team of surgeons, patients, and healthcare experts put patient safety first.

Richard Vazquez

MD, FACS

CEO/CMO




Dr. Vazquez practiced general surgery at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago for 40 years and created the SafeStart pre-invasive procedure system to eliminate never events and stop risk that harms patients, staff, and the facility. He remains Assistant Clinical Professor of Surgery and member of the faculty of the Feinberg School of Medicine at Northwestern University as an associate faculty member.

Dr. Vazquez is certified by the American Board of Surgery and is a Fellow of the American College of Surgeons. Dr. Vazquez is a serial entrepreneur and is the Founder-CMO/CEO/of SafeStart Medical, Inc. Dr. Vazquez remains affiliated at MATTER Chicago HIT incubator and frequently gives educational sessions on surgical safety.

Dr. Vazquez sits on the advisory boards of Xuron and Artificial Intelligence Healthcare Capital. He is currently the Director of Innovation and Entrepreneurship, Medical Informatics at Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine.

SPENCER L. BYRUM

Spencer L. Byrum is a founding member and the Chief Executive Officer of HRS Consulting, Inc., a premier human performance improvement company that was specifically created and designed to enhance individual and team decision making in high-risk industries. Mr. Byrum’s specialty is taking lessons learned in aviation and other dangerous occupations and developing innovative process improvements and training for professions in which people need to make critical decisions in a stressful, demanding environment.

Since becoming integrally involved in healthcare almost 20 years ago, Mr. Byrum has worked with over 600 healthcare facilities ranging from major tertiary care centers to Ambulatory Surgery Centers to Critical Access Hospitals. He has developed tools for the quantitative and qualitative assessment of the processes associated with the HAIs, SSIs, H&Ps, Informed Consent, Surgical Time Out, Surgical Site Marking, Surgical Counts, Medication Errors, and other processes that have been implemented by some of the largest healthcare institutions and companies in the country. Mr. Byrum has deep experience in assessing over 1,000,000 “near miss and sentinel events” and is an expert in evaluation of the causal factors and remediation of their impact. Finally, Mr. Byrum’s programs have been recognized by major insurers to reduce losses, leading to medical malpractice premium reductions for physicians and facility premium reductions for individual hospitals and related healthcare entities. Mr. Byrum is also the author of High Reliability for a Highly Unreliable World released in January 2018.

Richard H. Pearl

MD, FACS, FAAP, FRCS

Richard H. Pearl MD set a high bar for academic excellence, surgical innovation, and professional integrity through his 38 year clinical career and exemplary academic teaching. The US military recognized him with numerous military decorations and his peers have globally recognized his achievements in pediatric surgery across public and private health sector domains. Dr. Pearl completed a General Surgery Residency at Harvard Surgical Service, New England Deaconess Hospital in Boston Massachusetts and a Pediatric Surgery Fellowship at the hospital of Sick Children in Toronto, Canada, where he later became Chief of Pediatric Surgery. To name just a few of Dr. Pearl’s numerous academic and professional positions and honors, Dr. Pearl is Professor of Surgery and Pediatrics(Emeritus) at the University of Illinois College of Medicine in Peoria, and was Chief, Section of Pediatric Surgery, Attending Team Leader of the General Surgery Residency Pediatric Surgery Service, and Director of the Pre-Op Case Conference. He received the 2002-2003 UICOMP Faculty of the Year’ Award and the UIC all campus Faculty of the Year award as well. . The resident class of 2010 voted Dr. Pearl ‘Teacher of the Year’. For 20 years he was Children’s Hospital of Illinois Surgeon-in-Chief from 1998-2018 and directed Pediatric Trauma as well. Dr. Pearl is the father of three daughters, is recently retired from active practices but still serves on three boards at OSF and is active in activities at UICOMP.
